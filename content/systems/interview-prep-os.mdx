## The problem

Preparation for senior and executive interviews is one of the highest-stakes processes in a career, and almost everyone runs it as improvisation: cramming the night before, unstructured notes, no feedback loop. The process has no memory, no rehearsal infrastructure, and no way to compound across interview loops.

## The approach

Interview Prep OS turns preparation into an operating system — a cloneable template that gives any candidate a repeatable process:

- **Interviewer personas as sub-agents** — hiring manager, executive recruiter, VP of Engineering, VP of Design, and a live case interviewer, so a candidate can rehearse a full loop with calibrated pushback.
- **An orchestrated workflow** — a `/prep-interview` skill that runs research → mock interview → story-to-competency mapping → a per-loop prep one-pager.
- **A story bank as memory** — six to eight anchor stories in STAR form, mapped to the competencies each role actually screens for.
- **Company research that lands a strategic POV** — business model, competitor comparison, key metrics, driving toward a position on the gap the role would own.

## Where it fits the thesis

The same primitives, applied to a human process: memory (the story bank), orchestration (the prep workflow), governance (rubrics and calibrated feedback instead of vibes). It also demonstrates a belief that runs through all my work: any process people treat as an art usually contains a system waiting to be designed.
